Why hasn’t Blasted Science done anything lately?<\/strong><\/h3>\n

There’s no good answer to that question, since we spent most of that time just slacking off (which wasn’t unusual for\u00a0\u00be of the Blasted Science staff, I might add). Unfortunately, I personally spent a big chunk of that time working on a little project of mine.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Overset<\/strong><\/em><\/h3>\n

Overset<\/a><\/em> is a short adventure game that took about four months to make. However, it can be completed in less than fifteen minutes if you’re quick. That means I spent dozens of hours perfecting every single minute<\/em> of it.<\/em> Obviously, that makes Overset<\/em> one of the most polished games in history. I made the game for a contest. However,\u00a0I didn’t win, so I decided to upload it to the internet. For posterity, of course.<\/p>\n

Overset<\/em> is nothing short of a masterpiece. It features cutting-edge graphics and one of the best stories ever to grace the PC. It is absolutely flawless.<\/p>\n

Look at those spooky bathroom graphics!<\/p><\/div>\n

I’m lazy and I already wrote one description, so here’s the description from its Game Jolt page:<\/p>\n

\n
\n
\n
\n

Overset: to throw into confusion; disorder physically or mentally.<\/em><\/p>\n

Overset is a short adventure game that can be completed in less than an hour. Take control of a marble as you roll your way through almost 3 action-packed levels of low difficulty. Explore the dark world: a twisted reflection of the marble world. Uncover the secrets that probably exist there.<\/p>\n

That’s all there is to it. There’s nothing else to see here. Nothing at all. Definitely.<\/p>\n

Overset is a game about constantly defying your expectations. It was made for the 2016 STEM Video Game Challenge.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n
